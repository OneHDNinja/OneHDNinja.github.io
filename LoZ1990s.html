Their evolution
Though we take stunning graphics and intuitive UI for granted nowadays, videogames weren't always so robust and clever in their humble beginnings. Many of the earliest videogames could only output the most basic of graphics, had an '8-bit sound' instead of a high definition library of soundtracks, and often had buggy and fiddley control inputs that didn't always work and involved only mundane and repetitous button combinations that got tedious over time. Through innovation and progress over the decades, more realistic elements could be applied to videogames. Advances in computing allowed for 3D instead of 2D rendering, more space on a cartridge meant that more information could be stored in it, and therefore allowed for better graphics and higher fidelity sound, and lots more.
How they help people
People everyday go through troubling and stressful experiences that can leave them completely drained and out of energy by the end of the day. Whether it be work, financial troubles, a loss of a loved one, or things in general being too cumbersome, videogames have been developed as a fallback activity that people use to relax and relieve themselves of a hard day's work. They also help provide a sense of purpose and accomplishment to a person's life. Knowing in your head that you still have to go and save the princess or that you have yet to conquer and explore an entire map and complete a dungeon helps distract the mind form ordinary, mundane thoughts and provide some spice and diversity to spruce up someone's thoughts. They can also make an introvert or a shy individual break out of their shell and become a completely new, outgoing, and confident individual on the online world. On the more practical side of things, new advancements in VR technology by Oculus and HTC have opened the doors to development of certain simulators that can be used as a form of psychological therapy. Creating a game that simulates being at a great altitude or being on a public transport, and then immersing an individual who has problems with either of the two in VR, has been found to be significantly more effective than conventional methods at making their fears and irrational thoughts dissapear more quickly.
Their future
The videogame industry is a booming one, and its growth will not stop at any time soon. Like stated previously, videogames are receiving more and more exposure as it becomes a more adopted and widely accepted form of recreation. This means that new jobs will be created in the business and new people with their own talents, ideas, and innovations will join the game development scheme and keep advancing the progress at which videogames are developed and made better and better each day. New technologies and game development kits introuduced from graphics cards companies Nvidia and AMD will help developers make more real and immersive videogames that seem all the more alive. Additionally, coupled with the fact that technology gets faster roughly every one and a half years and its affordability becoming better and better as time goes by, more consumers will be able to purchase videogames that were once viewed as premium and hard-to-run content, which provides the videogame industry with more money, which in turn allows for better videogames to be produced. Overall, this creates a positive feedback loop that is self-sustaining and that is very unlikely to phase out of existence in the future.
